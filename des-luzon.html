LUZON

Luzon, the largest and most populous island in the Philippines, is a captivating blend of vibrant cities, breathtaking natural landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. From the bustling metropolis of Manila to the serene rice terraces of Banaue, Luzon offers a diverse range of experiences. Explore the majestic Mayon Volcano, hike through the lush forests of Mount Pulag, or immerse yourself in the vibrant traditions of the Ifugao people. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Luzon promises an unforgettable journey.

Places to Explore

Mayon Volcano
Albay

is a majestic stratovolcano known for its near-perfect conical shape. It's one of the most active volcanoes in the country, with frequent eruptions that have both captivated and threatened nearby communities. Its beauty and power make it a popular tourist destination, but also a constant reminder of the forces of nature.

Batad Rice Terraces
Banaue, Ifugao

Situated in the Cordillera region of the Philippines, the Batad Rice Terraces exemplify sustainable agriculture practices. These centuries-old terraces, meticulously maintained by local communities, demonstrate a harmonious relationship between humans and the environment.

Sumaguing Cave
South Rd, Sagada, 2619 Mountain Province

The cave network is vast and intricate, offering a thrilling challenge for experienced spelunkers. Steep descents and ancient stalagmites add to the adventure, revealing the cave's age and geological wonders. Its popularity among spelunkers speaks to its accessibility and the captivating experience it offers.
